Why Physical Media?

In our current technological era, streaming services dominate the way we consume media. Whether it's music, tv shows, or films, the convenience of on-demand access has made digital platforms the go-to choice for many; however, I am sure you may have come across an experience on a service like Netflix where a title you were looking forward to watching suddenly disappeared from the library. This is because streaming platforms operate on a licensing model, where content is only available for a limited time. When a license expires or a title is removed, it can no longer be accessed, leaving viewers without their favorite films.

When you purchase a Blu-ray or 4K UHD disc (or even vinyl and CDs), you own a tangible copy that cannot be revoked, altered, or removed from a digital storefront. Important now is censorship. With streaming services, studios can edit or remove content without notice, while physical media preserves the original experience. Collecting physical media also allows cinephiles to support filmmakers directly and enjoy bonus features that enhance the viewing experience.

Benefits of Physical Media

  • True ownership
  • Higher video and audio quality
  • Bonus features and commentaries
  • Collectible packaging and artwork
  • No dependency on internet access
  • Protection against content removal and censorship
